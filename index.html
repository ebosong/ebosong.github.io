Research Agenda

From foundation models to embodied decision making.

My research interest is centered on agents that must make decisions under uncertainty while moving through complex environments. The long-term goal is to build data-efficient, interpretable, and deployable autonomy stacks for drones, autonomous vehicles, and mobile robots.

01

Problem-driven autonomy

Start from concrete tasks such as long-range navigation, urban interaction, obstacle avoidance, and mission-level planning.

02

Learning with structure

Combine representation learning, dynamics priors, optimization, and safety constraints instead of treating autonomy as a black box.

03

Systems that can be tested

Build methods that can be evaluated in simulation, datasets, closed-loop benchmarks, and eventually real platforms.

Research Themes

Core directions

Embodied Intelligence

Learning agents that connect perception, memory, language, planning, and action through continuous interaction.

UAV Navigation

Robust aerial navigation for long-horizon missions, dynamic obstacles, GPS-denied settings, and uncertain environments.

Autonomous Driving

Scene understanding, behavior prediction, interactive planning, and safety-aware closed-loop decision making for road agents.

World Models

Predictive latent models for counterfactual rollout, uncertainty estimation, policy learning, and planning under partial observability.

Vision-Language-Action

Grounding natural language instructions in spatial reasoning, affordances, trajectories, and executable robot actions.

RL and Imitation Learning

Sample-efficient policy learning from simulation, demonstrations, offline logs, and online interaction.

Safety and Evaluation

Stress-testing autonomy through corner cases, uncertainty-aware prediction, formal constraints, and closed-loop metrics.

Datasets and Simulation

Reusable benchmarks, synthetic environments, sensor-fusion logs, and evaluation protocols for embodied decision systems.
